Halting Solution

Go to Project ->

The other day I was walking around NYC, completely parched, with an empty water bottle. I found it nearly impossible to figure out where the closest water fountain would be. Eventually, I discovered there were some commercial apps available, which seemed unnecessary considering the sheer amount of public municiple data available on the internet.

I had used Open Street Maps and Open Layers in the past to draw features on top of maps. I figured this would be an easy way to resolve some location data onscreen.

Initially I derived my data from the New York City Open Data Port, but found it remarkably difficult to work with for the data points I was extracting, and not particularly reliable. A friend recommended the Open Street Maps Tagging System, as it was community driven and open source. Using the front-end tool Overpass Turbo I was able to perform some basic queries, and download the result as geojson data (which is embedded directly into the page).

Known bugs
  • The circles are difficult to see and manipulate as you zoom in
  • Clicking to select items becomes unreliable on mobile devices
